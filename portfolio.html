Experience

CTO, Openlectures 2012-2014

Openlectures is a non-profit organization that strives to provide free, quality education by students, for students. I joined the tech team of 2 in early 2011, with the suggestion of a complete rewrite of their website in Ruby on Rails (back then RoR was hot, and the site still ran on a hacked-up Wordpress). Together, we completed the rewrite as well as a redesign in a few months. I took over as CTO soon after in 2012. Over the two year period, we tried lots of funky stuff, such as a Google spreadsheet to Postgresql synchronization platform. This allowed instructors to input new lecture data quickly.

Intern, Tinkertanker 2015-2016

I joined Tinkertanker in December 2015, after a 2 year stint in the army. I was tasked to build several applications, while given the freedom of choice of language and framework. I chose Clojure to build aagenda, a Slack inviter (with extra spice!), and a clone of Pidgeonhole for a client of theirs, and learnt much in the process.

Part of the job involved teaching kids of varying ages different levels of programming, from Scratch to basic Node.js. Through this I learnt how to convey technical knowledge to beginners in programming effectively.

Member, Fisherman 2016-present

I was one of the early adopters of Fisherman. Bucaran had approached me to try out this cool new shell snippet manager, and I loved cool and new stuff, so one could say he hit my soft spot. Since, I've produced several quality plugins that hundreds of people depend on.

This was my first real attempt at open-source work, and it has taught me how to make my code less fragile and more accessible to others.
